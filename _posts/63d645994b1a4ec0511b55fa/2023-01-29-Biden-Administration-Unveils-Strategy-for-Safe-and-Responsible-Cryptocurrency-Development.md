Biden administration outlines two-part plan for mitigating risks associated with cryptocurrencies. The White House released a statement on January 27, primarily directed at the US Congress. The Federal Reserve denied digital asset Custodia Bank membership in the Federal Reserve System

## Highlights

- The Biden administration's strategy for tackling the risks associated with cryptocurrencies was outlined in a statement released by the White House on January 27.
- The report presents a two-part plan for mitigating these risks.
- The first element of the plan is the comprehensive framework for digital asset development.
- The second element involves increased enforcement and new guidance from executive agencies.
- The statement mentions the creation of public awareness programs by government agencies to educate consumers about the risks of investing in cryptocurrencies.
- The Federal Reserve denied digital asset Custodia Bank membership in the Federal Reserve System on the same day the statement was issued, the day it was released.
