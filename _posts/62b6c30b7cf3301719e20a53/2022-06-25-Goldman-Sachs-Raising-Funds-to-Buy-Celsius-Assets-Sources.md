The Wall Street firm is seeking $2 billion in commitments from investors to buy distressed assets at steep discounts if the crypto lender goes bankrupt.

## Highlights

- Goldman Sachs is soliciting commitments from Web3 crypto funds, funds specializing in distressed assets and traditional financial institutions with ample cash on hand.
- The Wall Street firm is seeking $2 billion in commitments from investors to buy distressed assets at steep discounts if the crypto lender goes bankrupt.
- The assets, most likely cryptocurrencies having to be sold on the cheap, would then likely be managed by participants in the fundraising push, a source says.
- The firm is looking to tap into the money to buy the distressed assets if the lender fails to make a deal with the crypto-loaner, according to a person familiar with the situation.
