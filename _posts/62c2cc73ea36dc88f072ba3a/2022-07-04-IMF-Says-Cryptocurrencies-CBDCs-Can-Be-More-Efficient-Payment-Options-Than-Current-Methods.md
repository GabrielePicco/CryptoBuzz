The International Monetary Fund (IMF) has noted that digital assets can be effective alternatives to traditional finance products.

## Highlights

- The International Monetary Fund (IMF) has noted that digital assets can be effective alternatives to traditional finance products.
- The IMF said that some cryptocurrencies and central bank digital currencies (CBDCs) can be a more effective payment solution than credit and debit cards, especially on energy consumption.
- Previously, the IMF had maintained that the growth of cryptocurrencies poses a threat to financial stability.
- The body has released guidelines to develop a uniform global crypto regulatory framework.
- The policies mainly focused on establishing a level playing field for all crypto stakeholders.
- However, IMF chief economist Gita Gopinath opposed the idea of a blanket ban on cryptocurrencies but called for regulation of the sector.
