Former FTX founder and CEO Sam Bankman-Fried attempted to influence a potential witness in the case of FTX's collapse, prosecutors said.

## Highlights

- Prosecutors want to restrict Sam Bankman-Fried's access to encrypted messaging services.
- They say he was trying to influence a potential witness in the case of FTX's collapse.
- The disgraced CEO's current bond imposes no limitations in terms of contacting prospective witnesses in the ongoing case.
- He also made contact with other current and former FTX employees over email and via Signal, officials say.
- Prosecutors say he may have a history of using Signal to "obstructive purposes," according to court documents.
- The company collapsed in November after a $45 million hole in its balance sheet in the balance sheet, prosecutors say.
